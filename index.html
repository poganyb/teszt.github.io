A Historic Triple

Click to reveal fact

Estadio Azteca

Mexico City's legendary Estadio Azteca will become the first stadium in history to host matches in three separate FIFA World Cups (1970, 1986, and 2026).

Smart Travel Clusters

Click to reveal fact

Regional Groups

To reduce travel distances for players and fans, the host cities are grouped into three regional clusters: West, Central, and East. Teams will play group stage games within their cluster.

Record Attendance

Click to reveal fact

10+ Million Fans

With massive NFL and Liga MX stadiums hosting the games, FIFA projects over 10 million total ticket sales, smashing the previous record set by the USA in 1994 (3.6 million).

The Number 104

Click to reveal fact

More Games!

Originally planned for 80 matches, the tournament will feature 104 matches, meaning the champions must play 8 games (instead of 7) to lift the trophy.
